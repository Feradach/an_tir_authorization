Getting Started & Basic Navigation

Where can I find training videos?

In order to help you understand how the system works we have produced a set of training videos.

You can view these training videos here: Authorization Portal Training Videos.

How is this different from the old An Tir Lists authorization page?

The previous authorization system was mainly a public lookup tool that allowed users to find authorization records but not modify them. This portal includes that visibility, but also adds full workflow tools for members and officers. The primary goal is to provide a way for users to fully manage their authorizations within the online system and no longer require paper forms or email exchanges.

Major differences include:

  • Self-service account management (profile updates, password changes, linked child accounts, waiver-related steps).
  • Live authorization workflows with role-based approvals, rejections, and queue handling.
  • Supporting document uploads (including background check proof and equestrian waivers) with officer review tools.
  • Officer actions for appointments, sanctions, and notes with stronger permission enforcement.
  • Current-system reports and CSV exports from both reports and search pages.

How do I log in or recover my account?

Use the login page for normal sign-in. If needed, use the recovery link for username lookup or password reset instructions.

For security, recovery and login-instruction requests are rate-limited and privacy-protective. If you no longer have access to your email on file, contact the Kingdom Authorization Officer for help.

How do I find a specific person?

Use either method below:

  • Quick access: From the home page, use the fighter selector to jump directly to a fighter page.
  • Search pages: Use search filters (name, branch, discipline, style, and status controls) to find people and records.

What is the difference between "Table View" and "Card View"?

You can look at search results in two layouts:

  • Table View: Best for bulk review, sorting, and CSV export with the current filters.
  • Card View: Best for person-by-person review, especially on smaller screens.

You can switch between views from the search results page at any time.

How do reports and CSV downloads work?

The reports page pulls from current system data. You can generate reports and download CSV files from report tables. Search tables also support CSV download using your active filters across all result pages.

CSV files are exported with UTF-8 BOM to improve compatibility with Excel.

For Fighters

How can I view a fighter's authorizations and officer roles?

Open a fighter page from search or quick access. You will see identity details, branch, marshal officer roles, active/pending authorization records, and any actions your role allows. If it is your own profile, you can jump straight to account management.

How can I get a printable PDF of an authorization card?

Fighter pages provide card download buttons. The system generates separate PDFs by category:

  • Fighter Card for standard combat authorizations.
  • Youth Fighter Card for Youth Armored and Youth Rapier.
  • Equestrian Card for equestrian authorizations.

How do waivers and required documents affect authorization status?

Some authorizations need supporting requirements before they can become fully active. Depending on the workflow, you may see statuses like Awaiting Waiver, Awaiting Background Check, or Awaiting Equestrian Authorization Officer Review.

You can upload supported files (including background check proof and equestrian waiver documents) from account pages. For equestrian workflows, one roster-style upload can be linked to multiple fighters and multiple equestrian authorizations.

How do youth combat age categories work?

Youth Armored and Youth Rapier combatant authorizations are issued for the fighter's current age category:

  • Lion: ages 6-9.
  • Gryphon: ages 10-13.
  • Dragon: ages 14-17.

Each age category has its own authorization record for each actual fighting style, such as Youth Armored Weapon & Shield, Youth Armored Two-Handed, Youth Rapier Single Sword, or Youth Rapier Sword and Defensive Secondary. The system checks the fighter's birthday and will not issue a youth combatant authorization for the wrong age category.

Youth combatant authorizations expire at the earlier of two years from issue or the date the fighter ages out of that category. When a fighter moves into a new age category and receives the same actual style again, the previous age-category authorization for that style is marked inactive so only the current category appears as active in search results and on cards.

What are the An Tir equestrian authorization rules (quick summary)?

This is a practical summary of current An Tir participant authorization rules from the Book of Horse. Official policy text still controls if anything conflicts.

  • General requirement: You must be authorized for the activity you are doing, except supervised training/practice and other listed Book of Horse exceptions.
  • Ground Crew levels:
    • Ground Crew - Junior: minimum age 5.
    • Ground Crew - Senior: minimum age 16 and requires Ground Crew - Junior first.
  • General Riding: required base riding authorization for mounted activity; does not by itself authorize mounted weapons use.
  • Mounted Gaming: requires General Riding first.
  • Special mounted authorizations:
    • Mounted Archery, Crest Combat, Mounted Heavy Combat, and Jousting require Mounted Gaming first.
    • Mounted Heavy Combat requires both General Riding and Mounted Gaming.
    • Driving is a separate special authorization with its own skills test.
    • For first-time special authorizations, the Authorizing Marshal must also hold that same special skill.
  • Minor limits:
    • Age 5+ may authorize for General Riding, Mounted Gaming, Mounted Archery, and Ground Crew - Junior.
    • Age 16+ required for Ground Crew - Senior.
    • Minors cannot authorize for Crest Combat, Mounted Heavy Combat, Driving, Jousting, or Wooden Lance Jousting.
  • Authorization period: authorizations are valid for up to 4 years.

How does the Supporting Documents page work?

The Supporting Documents page is available from the main menu. Access is role-based:

  • Not logged in: page opens, but no documents are shown.
  • Logged in users: see documents related to their own account.
  • Kingdom Authorization Officer, Kingdom Earl Marshal, and Kingdom Equestrian Officer: can review all documents.

The same upload modal used on My Account is also available there.

How do I change my password or update my personal information?

Go to My Account from the main menu. From there you can update personal details, change your password, manage linked child accounts, and complete any upload/workflow items available to you.

Why is my membership expiration wrong?

The system only accepts membership information that can be validated against the current An Tir membership roster. Periodically, the Society sends An Tir a roster of current memberships held by An Tirians. The Kingdom Authorization Officer uploads that roster to the website, and the system updates membership expiration dates to the latest date found in the roster.

When you enter a membership number or expiration date, the system compares the following information to the roster and rejects the update if it does not match exactly:

  • The membership number.
  • The membership expiration date.
  • The legal first and last name, including differences such as a middle initial, extra name part, spelling variation, or recent name change.

If you recently renewed or changed your membership, the portal may not have the newest Society roster data yet. First confirm that the membership number, expiration date, first name, and last name you entered match your SCA membership account. If they match and the portal still rejects the update, contact the Kingdom Authorization Officer with proof of your renewal so they can review the roster data and make a manual correction when appropriate.

For Marshals

What can I do from the home page when I log in?

The home page acts as a role-aware dashboard. Marshals and officers see queue items and actions based on their authority, with document badges to quickly identify file state (No file, On file, or New upload where applicable).

How do I add a new fighter to the database?

If you have the required marshal authority, you will see an add-fighter action on the home page. The system then creates the person record and account through the normal onboarding workflow.

How do I add a new authorization for an existing fighter?

On the fighter page, use the add-authorization form. The system only shows disciplines/styles you are allowed to issue and validates requirements (such as age and prerequisites) before it creates records.

When acting as Kingdom Authorization Officer in specific workflows, use Approve As where required instead of direct self-approval.

How do the different authorization statuses work?

When authorizations are submitted they go through a series of checks and approvals before becoming active. Most of these are behind the scenes confirming that the marshal has the appropriate authorizations and that the fighter meets the requirements such as age or prior authorizations.

Some of the steps require additional actions from either the fighter or other authorized individuals. When that happens the authorization will be put put into one of several statuses. Each status has a specific circumstance in which it is used, specific actions required to clear it, and certain roles that can clear it.

The flowchart below shows the different statuses and lets you walk through a hypothetical authorization to see how they come into effect and get resolved. You can click on any individual status to see more details about it. If you click "Start" you can begin walking through the authorization process. At each step you will click on the line that represents the "choice" being made.

Sanctions are separate from the status flow. They temporarily hide affected authorizations from active displays while preserving the underlying authorization records.

How does the approval process for new Junior and Senior Marshals work?

Marshal authorization follows staged approvals with role-based actions and server-side checks:

  1. Proposal: A qualified marshal creates the pending record.
  2. Concurrence/Regional steps: Approvals are limited to authorized users in the correct scope (including region-scoped checks for regional actions).
  3. Kingdom steps (if required): Records may move into Awaiting Kingdom Authorization Officer Review or Awaiting Equestrian Authorization Officer Review, depending on workflow and settings.

Youth marshal flows can pause at Awaiting Background Check until a current check is on file. Updating background-check expiration can automatically advance those records.

For Officers

How do Regional Marshals know when to approve an authorization?

Your pending queue is filtered to what your office can act on. Regional approvals/rejections enforce region matching (including parent-region handling for local branches), so items only show where you have the right scope.

How do officer appointments and removals work now?

Appointment/removal permissions are role- and discipline-aware:

  • Kingdom discipline marshals can manage lower same-discipline offices.
  • Kingdom Earl Marshal can manage marshal offices except kingdom earl/auth officer roles.
  • Kingdom Authorization Officer can also manage Kingdom Earl Marshal and additional Kingdom Authorization Officer appointments.

Fighter pages hide appointment controls when the target already has an active office, and direct duplicate appointment attempts are blocked.

How do I manage marshal officer terms?

Use View Branch Marshals from the main menu. Eligible officers can view current appointments and take allowed term actions. Capability checks use the limiting date across membership, marshal status, and warrant validity.

As a Kingdom Authorization Officer, what can I do from the approval queue?

From the homepage queue and related fighter-page actions, Kingdom Authorization Officers can:

  • Turn Kingdom Authorization Officer verification on or off.
  • Bulk approve records currently in Awaiting Kingdom Authorization Officer Review.
  • Review and take allowed actions on Awaiting Background Check and Awaiting Equestrian Authorization Officer Review items.

If verification is turned off, existing Awaiting Kingdom Authorization Officer Review records are automatically processed through the normal approval flow.

How do I issue a sanction against a fighter?

Use the fighter page sanction action to open the sanction workflow. Sanctions are stored as dedicated sanction records with required end dates, and you can scope them by discipline/style based on your authority.

Kingdom discipline marshals can issue/lift within their discipline; Kingdom Earl Marshal and Kingdom Authorization Officer can issue/lift across disciplines.

How do I view or lift existing sanctions?

Use the sanctions management/search workflow to view active sanctions and lift them when authorized. Sanctions expire by end date and act as an overlay on authorization behavior rather than permanently rewriting underlying authorization records.

How do I update the "Officer Comments" on a user's profile?

On the fighter page, authorized officers can add notes/comments in the officer section. Notes now also capture the office or marshal status context used for the action, improving auditability.
